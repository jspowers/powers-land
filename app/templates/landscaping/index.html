Why Texas Native Plants?

Texas native plants are adapted to our local climate, soil, and wildlife. They require less water, fewer pesticides, and minimal maintenance while providing habitat for pollinators, birds, and other beneficial wildlife.

Benefits of Native Landscaping

Water Conservation

Native plants are drought-tolerant once established, reducing water usage by up to 50%.

Wildlife Habitat

Support pollinators, birds, and beneficial insects with plants they've evolved alongside.

Low Maintenance

Once established, native plants require minimal fertilizer, pesticides, or special care.

Year-Round Beauty

Native plants provide seasonal interest with blooms, foliage, and berries throughout the year.

Soil Health

Deep root systems prevent erosion and improve soil structure and water infiltration.

Climate Resilience

Adapted to Texas heat, cold, and variable rainfall patterns.
